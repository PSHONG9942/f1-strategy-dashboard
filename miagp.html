Superclipping & Lift-and-Coast

The 8MJ Requirement Flaw

The 50/50 power split relies heavily on a 350kW MGU-K. Originally, a massive 8 Megajoule (MJ) per lap harvesting target forced drivers to perform extreme "lift and coast" maneuvers hundreds of meters before braking zones, completely compromising the nature of qualifying.

Worse, "Superclipping"—where the MGU-K harvests energy from the ICE while the driver is full throttle mid-straight—was originally capped at a slow 250kW harvest rate. This meant long, unpredictable deceleration zones.

Suzuka Ramifications

Extreme speed differentials directly contributed to Oliver Bearman's heavy 50G impact with the barriers. Driver A deploying energy while Driver B was superclipping created massive, fatal closing speeds.

The "Macarena" Active Rear Wing

Ferrari SF-26 (The Original)

  • • Mechanism: Dual actuators hidden within carbon endplates.
  • • Rotation: Flips main flap by over 200 degrees.
  • • Aero Effect: Creates massive central hole, inverting profile to generate lift and drastically shed drag.
  • • Delta: ~10 km/h terminal velocity increase.

Red Bull RB22 (The Reaction)

  • • Mechanism: Central vertical actuator with straight extensions.
  • • Rotation: Limited to 110-120 degrees. Flap is pushed backward and lifted above endplates.
  • • Aero Effect: Mechanically simpler, but central actuator creates persistent airflow disruption (less efficient).
  • • Objective: Brute-force compensation for lack of mechanical grip.

The Solutions

On April 20, the FIA World Motor Sport Council executed a unanimous e-vote to bypass bureaucracy, implementing surgical technical directives designed to decouple driver cognitive load from battery software demands.

Qualifying Revolution

7.0 MJ

Harvest ceiling reduced from 8MJ. Drivers demand 12.5% less recuperation, eliminating lift-and-coast and restoring flat-out limit driving into braking zones.

Faster Regeneration

350 kW

Peak superclip power increased from 250kW. The battery reaches state-of-charge drastically faster, reducing the drag duration to a mere 2-4 seconds per lap.

Safety Mechanisms

Auto MGU-K

Low power start detection system automatically triggers MGU-K bursts if a car bogs down off the grid, preventing catastrophic trailing impacts.

Operational Overhaul: Extended FP1

Because of the massive upgrade packages and new energy algorithms, the FIA exceptionally extended Free Practice 1 from 60 minutes to 90 minutes.
